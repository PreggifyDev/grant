Obstetrics
🟢 Offline Ready
Eclampsia
Generalised seizures in a pregnant or recently postpartum woman - a life-threatening emergency.
Key Presentation
Tonic-clonic seizure in a pregnant or recently postpartum woman
Often associated with severe hypertension, though BP can occasionally be normal
Can occur antenatally, intrapartum, or postpartum up to 6 weeks
Risk of cerebral haemorrhage - the most common cause of death in eclampsia
Management Steps
!
CALL FOR HELP: Shout for help immediately. Get another pair of hands. Do not manage this alone.
1
CALL THE EMERGENCY TEAM AND SENIOR OBSTETRIC HELP IMMEDIATELY.
2
PROTECT THE AIRWAY: Left lateral position. Do not restrain limbs. Nothing in the mouth.
3
HIGH-FLOW OXYGEN: 15L/min via non-rebreathing mask once breathing resumes.
4
MAGNESIUM SULFATE: Loading dose 4g IV over 20 minutes. Then 1g/hour for 24 hours after delivery or last seizure.
⚠️ If seizures continue after magnesium
Give a further 2g IV bolus. If still uncontrolled, give IV diazepam 5-10mg slowly.
5
MONITOR FOR MAGNESIUM TOXICITY every 30 minutes: respiratory rate must stay above 12/min. Antidote: calcium gluconate 10mL of 10% IV slowly.
6
CONTROL BLOOD PRESSURE if above 160/110mmHg: IV labetalol or hydralazine. Target 135/85mmHg.
7
IV ACCESS: Two large-bore cannulae. Bloods: FBC, clotting, renal and liver profile, group and save.
8
CONTINUOUS FETAL MONITORING: CTG once the mother is stabilised.
9
PLAN DELIVERY: Eclampsia is an indication for delivery regardless of gestation once stabilised.
🔺 When to Escalate
Seizure continues despite two doses of magnesium sulfate
BP does not respond to two doses of antihypertensive
Patient loses consciousness or respiratory rate drops below 12/min
Signs of HELLP syndrome or DIC develop
🏥 If Resources Are Limited
No IV magnesium: Give diazepam 10mg rectally or IM and transfer urgently.

No labetalol: Use oral nifedipine (rapid-acting) 10mg, repeat after 30 minutes if BP still above 160/110.

No CTG: Auscultate fetal heart every 5 minutes with a stethoscope or Pinard.
▲
Involve the obstetrician or refer to a higher centre as soon as possible.

🚨 Outbreak Alert
NCDC
Cholera Outbreak: Lagos, Rivers, Kano
Federal Ministry of Health · 2 days ago
Situation Summary
Active cholera outbreak confirmed in Lagos, Rivers, and Kano states
847 cases reported, 23 deaths. Case fatality rate currently 2.7%
Contaminated water sources identified as primary transmission route
Healthcare facilities in affected areas to activate cholera response protocol immediately
Immediate Actions for Clinicians
!
ISOLATE all suspected cholera cases immediately on presentation.
1
REHYDRATE aggressively: ORS 75mL/kg over 4 hours for mild-moderate cases. Severe: Ringer's Lactate 100mL/kg IV over 3 hours (adults).
2
ANTIBIOTICS for severe cases only: Doxycycline 300mg single dose (adults) or Azithromycin 20mg/kg single dose (children and pregnant women).
3
NOTIFY your state epidemiologist of any suspected case within 24 hours.
📞 Report Suspected Cases
NCDC Emergency Operations Centre: 0800-970000-10
Report within 24 hours of suspicion - do not wait for lab confirmation
